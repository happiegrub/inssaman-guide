gameplay summary

The Inssa Man toy is a handheld character battling game. You raise and battle bizarre and quirky characters from the Inssa Man animated YouTube series. Your ultimate goal is to evolve your character from an adult to it's "King" and "God" forms.

Battles are effortless and the device does not have a care system. You do not need to feed, play or maintain your character's health and the character does not go to sleep. Your only tasks are to battle and expand your encyclopedia. To achieve evolution you must level your character, connect with other devices, collect and upgrade items and rebirth into multiple generations.

The gameplay loop is simple. You begin by waiting for your seed to grow into a baby. You can play mini games and feed your youngling to expedite it's growth into the next stage. Finally, when the character becomes an adult you can participate in battles. You will continue to battle until your character is max level. Once it's reached max level you unlock the ability to reincarnate. This will give you a new seed and you will repeat the process. You continue this loop for multiple generations until you meet the requirements for evolution.

growth stages


There are 5 different babies per device type (shell color) and each baby can grow up to be one of two adults. In other words, you can grow a total of 10 different adults per device. The adult your baby grows into is random.

    • gameplay tips


  • Write down or keep track of your current equips because you won't be able to see what they are if you're asked to replace them.
  • Furthermore, when receiving rewards after a battle you will only have a second to view equipment stats. The screen will then change and you will be asked if you want to replace the one you have in your item box. Since rewards are random, you could accidentally replace your +4 Sword with a +1 Sword. If equips are important to you, then make sure you're paying attention to rewards!

    menus and mechanics explained

    üß∞ item box
    1. Item Box
    1. Food
    2. Equips
    3. Evolution/Reincarnation Items
    		  
    1. Use this menu to feed your baby/junior. Select an item and press confirm to fill the hunger meter.
    2. Use this menu to equip your Inssa Man. Select an item and press confirm to equip it.
    3. Use this menu to view the number of evolution materials you own. You cannot directly interact with them.
  • Food and equips can be purchased with gold.
  • Food, equips, reincarnation materials and evolution materials can also be obtained as rewards from battles.
  • You can carry a max of 9 items per food type. In other words, you can have 9 Burgers, 9 Pizzas, etc. The quantity of each item will not exceed 9.
  • You only carry one of each equipment item. When you obtain an item you already own, you will be presented with the option to replace it.
  • Your Inssa Men can equip items when they reach the adult stage, but they can only equip one item at a time.

    üè™ item shop
    2. Item Shop
    1. Buy Food
    2. Buy Equips
    		  
    1. Use this menu to buy food. Select an item and press confirm to purchase the food.
    2. Use this menu to buy equips. Select an item and press confirm to purchase the equip.
  • You cannot carry more than 9999 gold.
  • You cannot carry more than 9 of each food item.
  • Buying equipment here will replace the one in your item box.
  • As an example, if you buy a +1 sword at the shop it will get rid of the +2 sword you have in your Item Box.
  • Buying a +1 sword at the shop does not make your +2 sword a +3 sword.
  • Your gold is deducted as soon as you accept the purchase. Be mindful when you buy an equipment upgrade. If you say no to the prompt or let the screen go to sleep you will lose the item and your gold!
  • The surprise box you can buy for 1000g will randomly give you one food (common), one evolution materials (rare) or one equip (very rare).

    3. Mini Games
    1. Rock, Paper, Scissors
    2. Balloon Pop
    		  
    1. Use A, B, or C to choose between rock, paper or scissors.

    ü•ä battles
    4. Battles
    1. CPU (General) Battles
    2. PVP (Communication) Battles
    		  
    1. Press confirm on this selection to initiate a CPU Battle.
    2. Use this menu to initiate a battle with another Innsa Man device.
  • Battles are expressed as auto battles. The battles carry out on their own and require no input from you.
  • Battles cannot be fast forwarded or skipped. You must wait for it to finish before initiating another one.
  • Your Innsa Man's stats determine how well they do in battle. Check the Character Info section for more info.
  • The numbers briefly displayed at the bottom left (your side) and bottom right (enemy side) during battle represent remaining health/max health.
  • At the end of the battle you will receive rewards (in this order):
    Gold -> Experience Points -> If any: Level Up -> If any: Stat Increases -> If any: Item Drops
    Gold rewards:  Win = 70g   Lose = 40g
  • Food, equips, reincarnation and evolution materials can be obtained as rewards from battles.
  • Characters can reach a maximum of lv. 30. Any exp gained after reaching lv. 30 is discarded.
  • CPU battles scale with your Innsa Man. If your adult is lv. 5 the opponent should also be roughly lv. 5.
  • Once your adult reaches lv. 30 they have a chance to encounter King and God Inssa Men in CPU battles.

    physical properties

  • The device requires 2 AAA batteries. You can open the battery cover with a Philips #1 or Philips #0 screwdriver.
  • Buttons are responsive and clicky.
  • Screen is vibrant. Brightness can be adjusted. Viewing the screen at various angles is acceptable.
  • Speaker is crisp. The highest volume is quite loud and lowest volume is very faint.
  • The volume can be adjusted, but the device cannot be muted completely.
  • There is only one bgm for the battles.
  • The soft plastic figurine can be wedged into a slot at the top of the device. The figurine is for aesthetics and does not affect gameplay.
  • The bottom of the device is curved so it cannot rest upright.
  • The device does not have a loop for phone charms or wrist straps.
  • Device body: roughly 5L x 6.5H x 3.2W cm. Screen Size: roughly 2.5W x 2.6H cm.
